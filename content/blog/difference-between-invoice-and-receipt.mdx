# Difference Between Invoice and Receipt — What Every Business Must Know

As a small business owner or freelancer in India, understanding the difference between an **invoice** and a **receipt** is fundamental. Both are important financial documents, but they serve very different purposes.

## What is an Invoice?

An **invoice** is a commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer **before or at the time of payment**. It acts as a **request for payment** or a **record of sale**.

An invoice contains:
- Details of goods or services provided
- Quantities and prices
- Tax breakdown (GST, if applicable)
- Total amount owed
- Payment terms and due date

## What is a Receipt?

A **receipt** is a document issued by a seller to a buyer **after payment has been made**. It acts as **proof of payment**.

A receipt contains:
- Amount received
- Date of payment
- Payment method (cash, UPI, cheque, etc.)
- Reference to the original invoice or transaction

## Invoice vs Receipt — Key Differences

| Feature | Invoice | Receipt |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Request for payment | Proof of payment |
| **Issued When** | Before/at time of supply | After payment received |
| **Who Issues It** | Seller/supplier | Seller/supplier |
| **Tax Details** | Yes (GST breakdown) | Usually not |
| **Legal Standing** | Triggers tax liability | Confirms transaction |
| **Used For** | ITC claims, accounting | Expense proof, refunds |

## When to Issue an Invoice

You should issue an invoice:

1. **Before payment** — For credit sales where payment is due later
2. **At the time of delivery** — For cash or immediate payment transactions
3. **For advance payment** — Issue a "Receipt Voucher" and then a Tax Invoice on supply
4. **For recurring services** — Monthly/quarterly invoices for ongoing contracts

Under GST, a **Tax Invoice must be issued within 30 days** of supply for goods and **45 days** for banking and financial services.

## When to Issue a Receipt

Issue a receipt:

1. **When cash is received** — Always issue a receipt for cash transactions
2. **When UPI/bank transfer is received** — As acknowledgment
3. **When advance is received** — Along with a Receipt Voucher under GST
4. **For retail sales** — Receipts (or simplified invoices) for daily retail transactions

## Can One Document Serve as Both?

Yes! In many retail and small business scenarios, a **simplified tax invoice** or **bill of supply** can function as both an invoice and a receipt once payment is made. This is common in:

- Restaurant bills
- Retail shop receipts
- Online purchase invoices

The key is that the document must clearly state the **payment has been received**.

## GST-Specific Rules in India

Under India's GST law:

- **Unregistered businesses** cannot issue a "Tax Invoice" — they issue a simple receipt/bill
- **Registered businesses** must issue a Tax Invoice for every taxable supply
- For **advance receipts**, issue a **Receipt Voucher** (under GST) until the actual supply
- Input Tax Credit (ITC) can only be claimed based on a valid **Tax Invoice**, not a receipt
