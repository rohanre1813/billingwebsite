Under the Indian Goods and Services Tax (GST) regime, issuing a tax invoice is not just a business best practice—it is a strict legal requirement. For a registered business to pass on the Input Tax Credit (ITC) to a buyer, the invoice issued must adhere to the specific format and rules laid out in the **CGST Rules, 2017**.

An incorrectly formatted invoice can lead to your clients being denied ITC, which can severely damage your business relationships. In this guide, we will walk you through the mandatory fields of a GST invoice and how to ensure you are 100% compliant.

## Is There a Fixed Layout?

The first thing to understand is that the Government of India **has not prescribed a rigid visual layout** for GST invoices. You are free to design your invoice, add your logo, and arrange the elements however you like. 

However, the government *has* prescribed a strict list of **mandatory fields** that must be present on the document. As long as those fields are clearly visible, your invoice is legally valid.

## Mandatory Fields on a GST Tax Invoice

According to Rule 46 of the CGST Rules, every tax invoice must contain the following 16 details:

### 1. Header Information
*   **Title:** The document must be clearly labeled as a "Tax Invoice".
*   **Invoice Number:** A consecutive serial number not exceeding 16 characters, in one or multiple series, containing alphabets or numerals or special characters (hyphen or dash and slash symbolized as "-" and "/"). It must be unique for a financial year.
*   **Date of Issue:** The exact date the invoice was generated.

### 2. Supplier (Your) Details
*   **Name:** Legal name of your business.
*   **Address:** Registered business address.
*   **GSTIN:** Your 15-digit Goods and Services Tax Identification Number.

### 3. Recipient (Buyer) Details
*   **Name & Address:** The buyer's name and billing address.
*   **Delivery Address:** Required if the delivery address is different from the billing address.
*   **Buyer's GSTIN / UIN:** If the buyer is registered under GST. If the buyer is unregistered and the value of supply is ₹50,000 or more, their name and address are mandatory.

### 4. Item Details
*   **Description of Goods or Services:** Clear details of what is being sold.
*   **HSN Code or SAC Code:** Harmonized System of Nomenclature code for goods, or Services Accounting Code for services. The number of digits required depends on your turnover.
*   **Quantity:** Total quantity and unit of measurement (e.g., kg, pieces, meters).
*   **Total Value:** The total value of the supply of goods or services.
*   **Taxable Value:** The value of the goods or services after taking into account any discount or abatement.

### 5. Tax Breakdown
*   **Rate of Tax:** The GST rate applied (e.g., 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%).
*   **Amount of Tax Charged:** The tax must be clearly split into:
    *   **CGST** (Central GST)
    *   **SGST** (State GST)
    *   **IGST** (Integrated GST)
    *   **Cess** (if applicable)
*   **Place of Supply:** Along with the name of the State, in case of an inter-state trade or commerce.

### 6. Authentication
*   **Signature:** Signature or digital signature of the supplier or his authorized representative. (Note: Under certain IT regulations, a computer-generated invoice without a physical signature is acceptable if it contains a disclaimer).

## What about a "Bill of Supply"?

A Tax Invoice is issued by a registered dealer who is charging GST. However, there are two scenarios where you cannot charge GST, and therefore cannot issue a Tax Invoice:
1.  You are registered under the **Composition Scheme**.
2.  You are selling **exempted goods or services**.

In these cases, you must issue a **Bill of Supply** instead of a Tax Invoice. A Bill of Supply looks identical to an invoice, but it does not contain any tax amounts (CGST, SGST, IGST) and does not allow the buyer to claim ITC.
